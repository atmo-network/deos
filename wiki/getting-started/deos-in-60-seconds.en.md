# DEOS in 60 Seconds

## The shortest version

DEOS is a forkable runtime framework for programmable economies. It connects token issuance, protocol-owned liquidity, routing, staking, governance, and automated actors into a deterministic institutional machine.

TMCTOL is the first economic standard on top of DEOS: a mint-only curve plus treasury-owned liquidity, fee burn, bucketed policy, and bounded governance control.

## The meme

DEOS replaces discretionary DAO treasury management with deterministic economic circuits.

Instead of asking a committee to improvise every treasury action, DEOS encodes reusable economic flows:

- Demand can mint through a curve;
- Issuance can build protocol-owned liquidity;
- Routing can choose market or protocol liquidity;
- Fees can feed burn, staking, or treasury lanes;
- Actors can wake from balances and execute bounded plans;
- Governance can tune explicit domains without pretending to own everything.

## Why it matters

Most token projects split economics, governance, liquidity, automation, and client truth across loose social promises. DEOS tries to make those relationships explicit inside the runtime and its surrounding validation stack.

That does not make markets predictable. It makes protocol reactions legible, bounded, and forkable.

## Who should care

DEOS is mainly for ecosystem founders, Substrate/Polkadot builders, tokenomics designers, protocol operators, and client/indexer builders who need a forkable foundation layer. It is not a finished retail app, a guaranteed demand engine, or a universal DAO platform.
